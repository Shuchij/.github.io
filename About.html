My Design Journey

Design was never very far from my world. I always enjoyed crafts more than art and needle work more than knitting. Professionally, I was introduced to design as a copy writer. Creative writing blended well with my hobbie of writing and my passion for travel. While I dedicated my intial writing career to travel writing and website content development, I always wanted to stay connected to the creative blend of writing. After recieving a job offer recommendation from a good friend I opted to make a career and a writing style change to technical writing. Unexpectedly, it was not technical writing that I got into. This writing was a blend of creative and technical writing styles, yet focussed more on the learning part. Instructional Design (ID) also known as learning design is a field of blended learning development, where technology and knowledge come together and user expectation always comes first. ID is a methedology that brings in instruction to the user through interactive content design. And this was the field that changed my perspective on design the most and moved me in the direction of human-interaction design.
